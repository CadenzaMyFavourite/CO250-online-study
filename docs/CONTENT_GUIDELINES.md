# Content guidelines

## Grounding

- Every formal definition/result and every procedure must have at least one supplied-source reference.
- Use PDF page numbers for review sheets and section numbers for the textbook when available.
- Never repair an unreadable formula by guessing. Mark it uncertain and inspect the rendered page.
- Generated questions must name their source concepts, origin, validation method, and validation status.
- “Source-verified” means checked against the cited statement or example, not formally proved by software.

## Explanations

Answer, where applicable: what it is, why it exists, assumptions, recognition signals, when to use it, when not to use it, common error, example, and prerequisite.

Keep the formal statement distinct from the explanation. Preserve mathematical notation. Never say “obvious”, “continue similarly”, or “by calculation” where the missing step is educational.

## Questions

- Vary reasoning direction and representation, not only coefficients.
- Hints progress from recognition to relevant result to first step.
- A full solution addresses the exact prompt and exposes decisive reasoning.
- Do not claim uniqueness without checking it.
- Proof review is heuristic unless a formal proof system was actually used.

## Review states

- `unreviewed`: extracted or drafted but not checked
- `machine-checked`: deterministic schema/calculation checks pass
- `manually-reviewed`: compared with the rendered supplied source
